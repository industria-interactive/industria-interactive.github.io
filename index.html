The Experience

INDUSTRIA: an interactive art & performance experience playfully reimagining life in the shipyard at the time of the industrial revolution.

The Illutron artist collective in partnership with a team of local and international artists-performers are proud to present Industria—an immersive arts experience taking place aboard the ship Illutron. The show will run in September for three weeks with dates including: 9/9 - 13/9, 16/9 - 20/9, and 23/9 - 27/9. With the purchase of a ticket for each specific time slot and date, visitors will be allowed to explore the performances and artworks aboard the ship according to their own desires for up to three hours.

Industria is a new form of sea borne immersive arts experience that combines elements of immersive performance, interactive art, and historical reimaging to create a multisensory world building experience that is part interactive gallery and part total theatre. With a storyline rooted in the Industrial Revolution and inspired by the novel Hard Times, Industria takes into account the history of Refshaleøen as an industrial area. Industria is a blend of real world and fiction, character and spectator, as well as past and present. Performances and installations can be found throughout the over 900 square meters of the barge which includes four levels, a vibrational sound room, a greenhouse dome, attached boats and platforms, and interior spaces from cabins to the large hull with bar area.

Our experience takes place aboard a 900 m2 barge known as Illutron.

Among other delights, installations will include: fire installations over the water, underwater lights, metalwork sculptures, pulse jet engine instruments, and a tesla coil instrument.

Illutron — The Ship

Illutron is a collaborative interactive arts studio established in 2007.

Illutron is located in Refshaleøen close to the Reffen street food market and Copenhagen Contemporary. Illutron creates large-scale interactive art, intriguing explorations into the realm where art and technology meet, experiments in the water and is a place for individual or collaborative work.

The organizers and artists who will bring Industria to life come from a rich background of performance and installation arts experience. At the helm is a team of three directors including: Keith Lim (Australia) performance artist and new technology artist, Mirabelle Jones (USA) creative technologist and former Senior Designer and Developer for Meow Wolf, and award-winning choreographer Kasper Ravnhøj (Denmark). The interdisciplinary artist team includes Copenhagen-based collective Cat Eyed People, sound objects from Lars Graugaard, as well as numerous Illutron artists.
